MATTER IS CONSERVED — The total number of each type of atom is the same before and after the reaction. Atoms are rearranged, not created or destroyed.
This is fictional! In real chemistry, this NEVER happens. Atoms cannot appear or disappear in a reaction — only rearrange.
📚 Key Vocabulary
Atom
The smallest unit of an element that keeps its chemical properties.
Molecule
Two or more atoms bonded together (like H₂O or CO₂).
Reactant
A substance that goes INTO a chemical reaction (the starting materials). Found on the left side of a chemical equation.
Product
A substance that comes OUT of a chemical reaction (what's made). Found on the right side of a chemical equation.
Chemical Reaction
A process where atoms in reactants rearrange to form new products.
Law of Conservation of Matter
In a chemical reaction, matter is neither created nor destroyed. The total number of each type of atom stays the same.
